### Per-case cost ≈ 4.6s on f64_idempotent

64 cases × 2 walks/case at supposedly ≤240 nanosecond steps per walk
should be sub-millisecond. We're seeing **5 orders of magnitude over
the plateau-width model.** Hypotheses, none confirmed:

- `shift_duration` does i128 saturating-add + two i128 divides per
  step (~hundreds of ns per step in debug Rust, but still <100µs
  per walk). Doesn't explain the gap on its own.
- Idempotence may exercise an outer-plateau path where the walk
  starts far from the convergence point — investigation deferred.

The plan (T5) caps `cases` on the slowest blocks to bring wall-time
into line. A deeper fix (replacing the walk with a closed-form
plateau-bottom computation) is **out of scope for this sprint** —
called out in the plan's Deferred section as a follow-up.
