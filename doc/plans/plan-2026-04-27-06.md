## Implementation

### Walks on the `Duration` rung

The shape mirrors `F064FD09` from the publish-prep sprint: walk on
the discrete rung (1ns increments here) and compare in float space.

The `def_walk_helpers!` macro from `conn::float` (landed with the
float-half sprint) generates `ascend_to_ceil` / `descend_to_ceil` /
`ascend_to_floor` / `descend_to_floor` from a `(src, dst, shift,
widen)` tuple. New helpers in `conn::time::duration`:

- `shift_duration(n: i32, d: Duration) -> Duration` — saturating
  ±n-nanosecond shift via `i128::saturating_add` on
  `whole_nanoseconds`. Reused by both bridges.
- `duration_to_f64(d: Duration) -> f64` — calls `as_seconds_f64()`.
- `duration_to_f32(d: Duration) -> f32` — calls `as_seconds_f32()`.

Two macro instantiations:

```rust
def_walk_helpers!(f64_durn_walks, f64, Duration, shift_duration, duration_to_f64);
def_walk_helpers!(f32_durn_walks, f32, Duration, shift_duration, duration_to_f32);
```

### Saturation arms (both bridges)

Derived from the `ExtendedFloat` lattice (`Bot < Extend(_) < Top`,
including `Top > Extend(f64::INFINITY)`):

| Input | `ceil` | `floor` |
|-------|--------|---------|
| `Bot` | `NegInf` | `NegInf` |
| `Top` | `PosInf` | `PosInf` |
| `Extend(NaN)` | `PosInf` | `NegInf` |
| `Extend(+∞)` | `PosInf` | `Finite(Duration::MAX)` |
| `Extend(-∞)` | `Finite(Duration::MIN)` | `NegInf` |

The asymmetric `±∞` arms are forced by Galois — `inner(PosInf)`
must equal `Top` (the synthetic-above-everything element), so
`ceil(Extend(+∞))` must be `PosInf`, while `floor(Extend(+∞))`
sits at the largest finite Duration.

NaN handling: under N5 (NaN ≤ Top, Bot ≤ NaN, NaN incomparable
with finite), `ceil(NaN) = PosInf` and `floor(NaN) = NegInf` are
the unique Galois-consistent assignments.

## Plateau & proptest budgeting

`F???DURN`'s walks step 1ns at a time on the `Duration` rung. The
"plateau" (range of Durations widening to the same float value) is
~ `f???_ULP × 1e9 ns` wide. For f64 at magnitude ≤ 1e9 s the
plateau is ~120 ns (negligible). For f32 at magnitude ≤ 10 s the
plateau is ~10⁴ ns (manageable). Beyond those magnitudes the
walks become infeasible for proptest.

Two width-specific generators bound the proptest input:

- `arb_duration_bounded_f64` — `Duration::new(s, n)` with
  `s ∈ -10⁹..=10⁹`. Used by `F064DURN` proptests.
- `arb_duration_bounded_f32` — `s ∈ -10..=10`. Used by `F032DURN`
  proptests.

The shared `arb_f32_bounded` is also tightened to `|x| ≤ 10` so
the float-side rung walks don't hit the f32 magnitude wall.

## Verification

For each Conn the standard Galois law battery from
`property::laws` runs at `cases: 64`:

- `conn_galois_l` / `conn_galois_r`
- `conn_closure_l` / `conn_closure_r`
- `conn_kernel_l` / `conn_kernel_r`
- `conn_monotone_l` / `conn_monotone_r`

Plus per-Conn deterministic spot tests covering: zero, an exact
mid-point, every saturation arm (Bot/Top/NaN/±∞/MIN/MAX), and the
plateau-bracketing case for `F032DURN.ceil(1.0_f32)` (which
returns the bottom of the plateau, *not* `Duration::seconds(1)`
exactly, because of the f32 rounding step).

## Deferred / out of scope

- **`F016DURN` / `B016DURN` (half-precision bridges).** `f16` ULP
  at magnitude 1 s is ~10⁻³ s ⇒ ~10⁶ ns of plateau per ceil/floor
  call; `bf16` is ~8× wider. The 1ns linear walk would take tens
  of millions of steps, infeasible under any realistic proptest
  budget. Revisit when a binary-search shift helper exists in the
  walk-helpers macro family.
- **`Weekday` / `Month` enum tags (`WDAYU008` / `MNTHU008`).** The
  `time` 0.3 crate doesn't impl `PartialOrd` for these enums, and
  the `Eq + PartialOrd` framework drives every law. Either upstream
  PR or local newtype wrappers are required first; both are
  out-of-scope for this Phase B MR.
- **`UtcOffset` (`UTCOSECS`).** `UtcOffset`'s derived `Ord` orders
  by packed bytes, not by `whole_seconds()` — so the lattice
  predicates would assert against an unintuitive total order.
  Needs a newtype wrapper for the same reason as Weekday/Month.
